3. Purpose of Meeting  
The chair stated the meeting’s purpose was to vote on the funding model for structural and maintenance 
repairs, based on recommendations from the Pentas Group reserve fund study and engineering input from 
DKM Engineering.  
 
4. Presentation  —  Pentas Group (Clark Bezo)  
Clark Bezo (Pentas Group) summarized:  
• 
Completed work:  rafter repairs; shingling on three of four buildings; plywood replacement where rot 
was found; chimney work on decommissioned chimneys (including crickets installed to deflect water).    
• 
Outstanding items / follow -ups: chimney flashing repairs; remaining chimney decommissioning; crawl  -
space moisture (attributed to ground seepage rather than roofing failure); furnace   -room moisture 
issues (likely condensation/insulation/venting related), with some questions deferred to management 
or the board.  
 
• 
Reserve fund balances and contractor payments:   questions about reserve fund discrepancies and 
payments while awaiting loan draws; management explained reserve funds were used temporarily to 
pay obligations while financing was pursued.   
• 
Governance and board changes:  owners attempted to raise motions regarding board changes; the 
chair ruled these out of order due to the agenda being limited to the funding vote. Management noted 
that between AGMs, owners in good standing may be appointed to open board positions and asked 
interested owners to submit names for consideration.   
• 
Lien status:  it was stated there was no registered construction lien, based on legal advice referenced 
during the meeting.

5. Funding Options and Main Motion 
Two options were presented: 
1.   Option 1: Obtain an approximately $1.3 million loan, amortized over about 20 years, with a condo fee 
increase to support repayment. 
2.   Option 2: Proceed by special assessment/levy, estimated at approximately $17,000 per unit over 12–
18 months. 
 
6. Voting Process and Result 
• 
Voting was conducted by private Zoom poll, using unit factors and a vote-tracker sheet to reconcile 
results. 
• 
Outcome: Option 1 (loan) carried overwhelmingly (~96% support). 
 
7. Unfinished Business / Follow-Up Items 
•

•

issues. 
• 
Sidewalk repair bundling and SaskEnergy reimbursement status. 
• 
Owner requests for documentation and inspection reporting. 
• 
Process for submitting names for interim board appointment consideration. 
 
8. Adjournment 
The Chair indicated the meeting would be concluded at approximately 1:43 PM. The meeting ended after the 
announcement of the vote results, with no further business within the adopted agenda.
